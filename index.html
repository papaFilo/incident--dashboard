Executive Summary

On June 25, 2026, at 11:00 WIB, a thermal incident occurred on a backup Diesel Generator unit owned and operated by PT. Unisat at the Gemah#4 Pad (GE#4 Pad). The generator was put into service on June 22, 2026, as a backup replacement for the main generator that had tripped the previous day. This deployment was part of an immediate action to minimize Loss Production Opportunity (LPO) from the Shut-In (S/I) well by restoring primary electrical power to the GE-6 ESP system. Upon detecting the incident, on-site HSSE personnel responded promptly by utilizing Portable Fire Extinguishers (APAR), successfully containing and extinguishing the localized flame.

Root Cause Chain Reaction

1. Pulley Broken
Structural failure / minor micro-cracks on the fan belt pulley component.
2. Unbalance Rotation
The pulley damage causes eccentric and unstable high-RPM rotation.
3. Fan Belt Broken / Parted
The extreme unbalance rotation snaps the cooling fan drive belt.
4. Extended Overheat
Engine operated without forced cooling, driving critical block temperatures high.
5. Safety Device Malfunction FAILED
The emergency shutdown (ESD) system failed to automatically kill the engine.

Fire Triangle Analysis

The extended overheating triggered thermal degradation of equipment materials, completing the ignition criteria:
